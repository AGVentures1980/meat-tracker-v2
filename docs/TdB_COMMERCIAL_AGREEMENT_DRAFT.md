### PART 1: THE 90-DAY PERFORMANCE PILOT AGREEMENT
*(To be entirely risk-free for the Client, based solely on verified operational savings)*

**1. OBJECTIVE & SCOPE**
This document outlines the deployment of the Brasa Meat Intelligence OS (including the Master Ledger, Offline PWA Inventory, and "Garcia Rule" compliance engine) in a controlled pilot environment to benchmark and validate Food Cost reduction and operational margin recovery.

- **Pilot Duration:** 90 Days from the official "Go-Live" date.
- **Pilot Scope:** Three (3) selected Texas de Brazil locations (e.g., Dallas Main, Fort Worth, Addison).

**2. FINANCIAL STRUCTURE (THE PERFORMANCE MODEL)**
During the 90-Day Pilot phase, AGV Ventures (Provider) assumes the performance risk. The pricing structure is strictly tied to verified operational savings.

- **Implementation Fee:** $0.00 (Waived for the Pilot).
- **Software Licensing (SaaS) Fee:** $0.00 / month (Waived for the Pilot).
- **Performance Participation Fee:** 8% of the Verified Margin Savings generated during the 90-day period across the 3 pilot locations.

*If no verifiable savings are generated during the pilot period comparing the pilot data against the "Frozen Baseline", the performance fee shall be $0.00.*

**3. THE "FROZEN BASELINE" (THE REFERENCE POINT)**
To accurately calculate the 8% Performance Participation Fee, both parties must agree on the historical operational metrics before the Brasa OS goes live.

- **Baseline Metrics Required (per pilot store):**
  1. Average Weekly Protein Yield (%) trailing 6-12 months.
  2. Average Weekly Food Cost (%) trailing 6-12 months.
  3. Average Lbs Consumed per Guest trailing 6-12 months.
  4. Average Weekly Overproduction Variance (Theoretical vs. Actual).

*(These numbers will be documented in "Appendix A: The Frozen Baseline" and signed by both parties on Day 1).*

**4. TRANSPARENCY & AUDIT RIGHTS**
To calculate the savings accurately, the Provider requires continuous, read-only access to the necessary ground-truth data during the pilot.
- Client agrees to provide weekly or automated access to POS (Micros/Aloha/Olo) guest counts and ERP/Redbook invoice costs for the specific test proteins to allow the Brasa OS to calculate the ongoing ROI.

### PART 2: THE ENTERPRISE ROLLOUT (POST-PILOT S.A.A.S. AGREEMENT)
*(To be automatically activated upon successful validation of the Pilot goals)*

**1. THE ACTIVATION TRIGGER**
If the 90-Day Pilot demonstrates a verified annualized saving trajectory of **[INSERT AGREED MINIMUM TARGET HERE, e.g., $150,000 / year across 3 stores]**, this Enterprise Rollout agreement shall automatically activate for the remaining Texas de Brazil network (e.g., 57+ stores).

**2. ENTERPRISE FINANCIAL STRUCTURE**
Upon validation of the technology as a core margin infrastructure, the relationship transitions to a scalable Enterprise Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) model.

- **One-Time Implementation & Network Onboarding Fee:** $50,000.00
  *(Covers full network database structuring, Master Ledger configuration, data ingestion templates, and corporate-wide digital training/certification).*
- **Enterprise SaaS Licensing Fee:** $349.00 per store / month.
  *(Billed annually or quarterly. Covers cloud hosting, high-availability mobile access, offline PWA syncing, and standard technical support).*
- **Scale Performance Participation Fee:** 5.0% to 6.0% of Verified Margin Savings.
  *(Reduced from the 8% pilot rate, ensuring the vast majority of the recovered margin remains with the Client's EBITDA).*

**3. SYSTEM GOVERNANCE & ACCOUNTABILITY ("THE GARCIA RULE")**
Under the Enterprise Agreement, the Client acknowledges that the Brasa OS employs strict accountability mechanisms.
- Store Managers will be subject to operational lockouts if mandatory inventory checks (e.g., Sunday night cold-room audits) are not completed.
- Master pricing is exclusively governed by corporate via the Master Ledger. 

**4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY & DATA OWNERSHIP**
- **Platform IP:** The Brasa OS algorithms (including "The Garcia Rule", "Idea Vault", and Smart Prep formulas), source code, and user interfaces remain the exclusive intellectual property of AGV Ventures.
- **Client Data:** All raw sales, inventory, and cost data inputted into the system remain the exclusive property of Texas de Brazil.
